# Scale & Unit Economics Calibration

Use this pillar to score how the business behaves after the first few users.

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | High gross margin, recurring revenue, low support load, clear LTV/CAC >5, and scale improves the product or distribution. |
| 7-8 | Healthy subscription or repeat revenue, support is bounded, CAC is plausible, and founder can reach meaningful MRR solo. |
| 5-6 | Economics can work, but growth depends on manual sales, modest ARPU, or a scale ceiling that appears within one year. |
| 3-4 | One-time revenue treadmill, low margin, high support, expensive acquisition, or churn that eats the base. |
| 0-2 | Each customer loses money, requires custom labor forever, or cannot scale beyond a small service business. |

Red flags that force a score <=2:

- No credible buyer pays enough to cover support.
- The product needs custom work for every customer with no recurring fee.
- CAC is unknown and likely larger than first-year revenue.
