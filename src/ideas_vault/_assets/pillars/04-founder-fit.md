# Founder-Fit Calibration

Use this pillar to score fit with your real constraints. A great idea for a different founder can be a bad idea for you.

| Score | What it looks like |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | It uses your strongest skills, sells to an audience you already reach, fits your budget, and gives energy for 6-12 months. |
| 7-8 | Mostly aligned. One gap exists, but it is learnable or can be handled by a narrow scope, contractor, or partner. |
| 5-6 | Neutral fit. You can build it, but distribution, motivation, or domain credibility is not clearly yours. |
| 3-4 | Major mismatch: sales channel, language, geography, trust, budget, or core skill gap is outside your current leverage. |
| 0-2 | The idea depends on a constraint you cannot change soon, such as licenses, local presence, enterprise access, or large capital. |

Red flags that force a score <=2:

- You have no reachable path to the target customer.
- The idea requires a skill you do not want to learn and cannot afford to hire.
- The work style conflicts with the life or company you are trying to build.
