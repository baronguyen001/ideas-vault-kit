# 04 - Founder-Fit

## Core question

Does this idea fit an automation-leaning solo developer with no existing restaurant-sales channel?

---

## Example Founder Profile

- **Role**: solo technical founder
- **Strongest skills**: web automation, API integration, scripting, lightweight SaaS
- **Weak skills**: local B2B sales, native mobile polish, in-person onboarding
- **Network / audience**: developers, online builders, small software operators
- **Budget / runway**: bootstrap budget under $500/mo
- **Weekly hours**: 15-25 hours
- **Timezone / languages**: async online channels preferred

---

## 4.1 Skill Match

| Idea requires | Founder has | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| Web app / PWA build | Strong | Small |
| Native iOS polish | Weak | Large |
| Payment integration | Medium | Medium |
| Restaurant operations | Weak | Large |
| Local sales and onboarding | Weak | Large |

## 4.2 Sales Channel Match

The buyer is a restaurant owner or operator. The strongest channels are local referrals, in-person visits, phone calls, and existing relationships with hospitality businesses. That is a poor match for a founder whose audience is mainly online builders.

## 4.3 Budget & Runway

The budget is manageable for a prototype, but customer acquisition takes time. Local sales experiments, demos, and support consume the scarce resource: founder hours.

## 4.4 Time to First Revenue

Realistic time to first dollar: 60-120 days without an existing local network. That is too slow for a small service bet unless the founder already has restaurant leads.

## 4.5 Energy Match

This idea requires hands-on client work, support urgency, and repeated sales conversations. It is not naturally aligned with an automation-first builder who prefers async software leverage.

## 4.6 Bull vs Bear

**Bull case**:

The founder can build templates and automate parts of delivery faster than a traditional agency.

**Bear case**:

The hard work is not code. It is trust, sales, onboarding, and support in a market the founder does not already reach.

---

## Score: 5/10

**Reasoning**:

The build side partly fits, but the channel and service model do not. This should be evaluated as a local agency, not a software product.
