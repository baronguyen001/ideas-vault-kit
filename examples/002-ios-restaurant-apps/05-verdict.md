## Verdict: **NO-GO**

## Reasoning

The idea is a NO-GO in its original form. The viral claim makes app generation sound like the business, but the business is actually restaurant sales, onboarding, payments, App Store compliance, and ongoing support. Apple Guideline 4.2.6 undermines the "many template apps under one service" model, and the market is already crowded with cheaper alternatives. A pivot may work, but the native iOS app pitch should be killed.

## Top Risks

1. App Store rejection or account setup friction blocks delivery.
2. Restaurants choose cheaper POS, ordering widget, or website alternatives.
3. Support workload grows faster than recurring revenue.

---

## Pivot Options

1. **PWA ordering page**: sell responsive ordering pages with no App Store promise.
2. **Restaurant marketing bundle**: menu copy, photos, Google profile, and social posts.
3. **Agency tool**: build templates for local agencies instead of selling directly to restaurants.

---

## Next Steps If GO

This is not a GO. If testing a pivot, start with 10 restaurant owner conversations before building.

## Kill Criteria

- Any App Store rejection tied to template-app policy.
- Fewer than 2 qualified leads after 50 targeted local outreach attempts.
- Support time exceeds 3 hours per restaurant per month without recurring fees.
