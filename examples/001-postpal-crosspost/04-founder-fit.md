# 04 - Founder-Fit

## Core question

Does this idea fit a generic automation-leaning solo developer with a small build-in-public audience?

---

## Example Founder Profile

- **Role**: solo technical founder
- **Strongest skills**: web apps, automation, API integrations, writing technical notes
- **Weak skills**: enterprise sales, consumer brand design, paid acquisition
- **Network / audience**: indie builders, developer communities, small SaaS founders
- **Budget / runway**: bootstrap budget under $500/mo for tools and hosting
- **Weekly hours**: 15-25 hours
- **Timezone / languages**: async-friendly, English-first online channels

---

## 4.1 Skill Match

| Idea requires | Founder has | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| OAuth + APIs | Strong | Small |
| Scheduling backend | Strong | Small |
| Markdown UX | Medium | Small |
| Creator marketing | Medium | Medium |
| Platform policy handling | Medium | Medium |

## 4.2 Sales Channel Match

The target customer hangs out in developer Twitter/X, Mastodon, Indie Hackers-style communities, Hacker News side threads, and devtool founder circles. A builder who posts publicly can dogfood the product and show workflow screenshots.

## 4.3 Budget & Runway

MVP cost fits a small bootstrap budget. The only budget risk is paid API access if the product depends heavily on X.

## 4.4 Time to First Revenue

Realistic time to first dollar: 45-75 days. This is acceptable because a limited beta can be sold before the full suite exists.

## 4.5 Energy Match

The work is aligned with building in public: writing, automation, launch notes, and small SaaS craft. It does not require offline sales or enterprise trust.

## 4.6 Bull vs Bear

**Bull case**:

The founder can be the first user, publish the build process, and reach the exact persona.

**Bear case**:

If the founder dislikes social posting or stops dogfooding, the distribution advantage disappears.

---

## Score: 7/10

**Reasoning**:

Strong technical and channel fit for an automation-oriented builder. Not a 9 because the product still needs consistent content marketing and platform policy patience.
