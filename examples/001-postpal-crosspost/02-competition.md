# 02 - Competition & Market

## Core question

Is there room beside Buffer, Typefully, and Hypefury for a narrow markdown-first tool?

---

## 2.1 Market Validation

### Demand signals

- Social scheduling is a proven paid category with multiple long-running products.
- Indie builders and creators already write launch updates, changelogs, and build-in-public posts.
- Markdown is common in developer workflows, but most social tools optimize around visual calendars and dashboards.
- Demand is not hypothetical; the question is whether the wedge is sharp enough.

### Market sizing

- TAM: broad social media management software, too large and generic to matter here.
- SAM: solo founders, indie developers, technical creators, and small product teams.
- SOM for a solo founder in 3 years: 500-1,500 paid users is plausible if the niche converts.

### Market status

Selected: **validated**

There are several credible competitors, users pay for the job, and no single tool owns "markdown-first build-in-public cross-posting."

---

## 2.2 Competitor Landscape

| Name | Pricing | USP | Traction | Weakness | Source |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| Buffer | Free / paid tiers | Broad social scheduling | Established brand | Generic; not markdown-first | [Buffer pricing](https://buffer.com/pricing) |
| Typefully | Free / paid tiers | Strong X and LinkedIn writing workflow | Popular with creators | Less focused on markdown file workflows | [Typefully pricing](https://typefully.com/pricing) |
| Hypefury | Paid tiers | Creator automation and growth features | Established in creator market | Heavier than a simple dev workflow | [Hypefury pricing](https://hypefury.com/pricing) |

### Indie / stealth competitors

Many side projects exist around "write once, post everywhere." Treat this as validation, not a moat. The product should win with focus, not breadth.

### Adjacent substitutes

| Name | How customers use it instead |
|---|---|
| GitHub discussions / changelog | Publish in one place and manually repost snippets |
| Notion / Google Docs | Draft centrally, then copy-paste manually |
| Zapier / Make | Automation glue, but weak preview and editing workflow |

### Big tech threat

Platforms may add better native scheduling, but cross-platform markdown workflow is unlikely to become a priority for each platform.

## 2.3 Niche Carve-Out

| Angle | Applies? | Specific niche |
|---|---|---|
| Vertical | Yes | Indie software builders and devtool teams |
| Geography | No | Not the wedge |
| Persona | Yes | Builders who write updates in markdown |
| Price wedge | Somewhat | Simpler $9/$19 plans under heavy suites |
| Channel / UX | Yes | File-first, markdown-first, keyboard-friendly |
| Bundle | Yes | Drafting + preview + scheduling + link hygiene |
| AI-first | Optional | AI rewrite suggestions later, not MVP |

One-line differentiator:

> Markdown-first cross-posting for indie builders who want one source of truth for launch updates.

---

## 2.4 Pricing Power

- Free: 1 profile set, 10 scheduled posts.
- $9/mo: solo builder, 3 profile sets, 100 scheduled posts.
- $19/mo: small team, shared calendar, analytics export.

- [x] Founder unfair advantage if the founder already ships in public

## 2.6 Bull vs Bear

**Bull case**:

The market is validated and the buyer persona is reachable through public builder communities. A narrow tool can feel much better than a generic scheduler.

**Bear case**:

The space is crowded, and platform API costs can push the product toward the same pricing and complexity as incumbents.

---

## Score: 7/10

**Reasoning**:

Competition is real but helpful. The score depends on keeping the wedge specific: markdown-first for builders, not "another social media scheduler."
